# Model Comparison

## Model 1 – Marketing Spend

Monthly Sales is the dependent variable.
Independent Variable: Marketing Spend
- R²: 0.167
- P-value: < 0.001

### Interpretation

Marketing expenditure is statistically significantly positively related to monthly sales. The model accounts for only 16.7% of the variance in sales, however, suggesting that there may be other factors which affect store performance.

---

## Model 2 – Footfall

The dependent variable is the monthly sales.
- Independent Variable: Footfall
- R²: 0.736
- P-value: < 0.001

### Interpretation

The extent of foot traffic is a much more reliable indicator of the monthly sales and drives about 73.6% of the sales variation.

---

## Model 3 – Multiple Regression

Variables Included:

- Marketing Spend
- Footfall
- Inventory Availability
- Customer Rating
- Region (East Dummy)

R² = 0.817

### Interpretation

Multiple regression model is the best model to explain monthly sales and it includes all the operational, marketing and customer related variables.

## Final Model Selected

The multiple regression model was chosen because it was the most explanatory model (R² = 0.817) and it contains multiple important business drivers.

## Limitations

Association is only detected by regression, not causation. Other factors like seasonal variations, local events and competitor activities can also affect monthly sales.
